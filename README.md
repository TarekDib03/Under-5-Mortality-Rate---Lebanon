# Under-5 Mortality Rate in Lebanon (2019–2024): A Public Health & MEAL Analysis

**Overview**

This project was developed as part of the Epidemiology in Public Health course offered by Johns Hopkins University through Coursera. The analysis examined the reversal of Lebanon’s Under-5 Mortality Rate (U5MR) trend between 2019 and 2024 and explored how overlapping economic, health, and humanitarian crises contributed to worsening child health outcomes.

The project combined epidemiological analysis, Monitoring & Evaluation (M&E) framework design, logic model development, and problem-solving methodologies to assess structural determinants affecting vulnerable populations, particularly refugee and impoverished Lebanese children.

**Project Objectives**

The project aimed to:

- Analyze the reversal of Lebanon’s Under-5 Mortality Rate trend
- Identify key determinants contributing to increased child mortality
- Examine inequities between Lebanese and refugee populations
- Design an evidence-based M&E framework for child health interventions
- Develop a results-based logic model to support humanitarian and public health programming
- Generate actionable recommendations for policymakers and humanitarian actors

**Possible Contributing Factors**

Several interrelated crises have contributed to this increase:

- Economic Collapse (2019–present): The sharp deterioration of the Lebanese currency and prolonged economic crisis have drastically reduced household purchasing power, limited access to health services, and increased poverty and food insecurity.
- COVID-19 Pandemic: The pandemic further weakened an already fragile healthcare system, disrupted routine health services, and worsened socioeconomic conditions.
- Healthcare System Strain: Limited resources, shortages of medical supplies, and reduced access to maternal and child health services have constrained essential care.
- Syrian Refugee Crisis: Lebanon hosts a large refugee population, placing immense pressure on already-strained health, education, and social services.
- Living Conditions: Refugees, in particular, face overcrowded housing, poor sanitation, and limited access to adequate nutrition and healthcare, heightening risks for preventable diseases.

**Monitoring & Evaluation Framework Design**

As part of this project, I designed the Monitoring & Evaluation (M&E) framework and analytical problem-solving methodology used to assess child mortality trends and intervention priorities.

The framework included:

- Indicator identification and tracking
- Outcome and impact measurement approaches
- Disaggregated data analysis
- Evidence interpretation methodologies
- Equity-focused monitoring considerations
- Recommendation development processes

The approach integrated epidemiological analysis with evidence-based decision-making principles to strengthen adaptive programming and intervention targeting.

# Logic Model Development — Safe Start, Brighter Future

To support strategic planning and results-based programming, I developed a comprehensive logic model titled:

Safe Start, Brighter Future

**Overall Goal**
Reduce Under-5 Mortality Rate (U5MR) among refugee and impoverished Lebanese children.

**Results-Based Framework Structure**

**Inputs**
- Funding
- Human Resources
- Infrastructure
- Medical Supplies
- Partnerships
- Community Networks

**Activities**
**Healthcare Services**
- Mobile clinics
- Primary healthcare center strengthening
- Vaccination campaigns
- Medical referrals
- Staff incentives
**Maternal & Child Health**
- ANC/PNC services
- Skilled Birth Attendance (SBA) promotion
- Community Health Worker (CHW) training
- Home visits
**Nutrition Interventions**
- Infant and Young Child Feeding (IYCF) counseling
- Growth Monitoring & Promotion (GMP)
- OTP/TSFP nutrition programming
- Micronutrient distribution
**WASH Interventions**
- Hygiene promotion campaigns
- Hygiene kit distribution
- Sanitation infrastructure support
**Community Awareness**
- Community health education
- CHW deployment
- Awareness sessions
- Community mobilization

**Outputs**

Examples of measurable outputs included:
- Number of PHCs supported
- Number of vaccines administered
- Number of ANC/PNC visits
- Number of children screened for malnutrition
- Number of hygiene kits distributed
- Number of CHWs trained
- Number of awareness sessions conducted

**Outcomes**
**Improved Health Utilization**
- Increased vaccination coverage
- Increased skilled birth attendance
- Increased healthcare-seeking behavior
**Improved Nutrition**
- Increased exclusive breastfeeding
- Reduced acute malnutrition
- Improved dietary diversity
**Reduced Morbidity**
- Reduced diarrheal diseases
- Reduced respiratory infections
- Reduced vaccine-preventable diseases

**Long-Term Impact**
- Reduction in Under-5 Mortality Rate (U5MR)
- Improved child survival and well-being
- Strengthened resilience among vulnerable populations

**Epidemiological and MEAL Insights**

The reversal of the U5MR trend illustrates how overlapping health, economic, and political crises can erode decades of public health gains. It also highlights the importance of disaggregated data to capture health inequities between host and refugee populations.

The project highlighted the importance of:

- disaggregated data collection
- refugee-sensitive monitoring
- equity-focused programming
- integrated humanitarian approaches
- adaptive and evidence-based decision-making
- results-based management frameworks

**Recommendations**

**Strengthen Health Systems**
- Expand access to maternal and child healthcare services
- Improve the availability of medicines and medical supplies
- Strengthen primary healthcare infrastructure

**Refugee-Focused Interventions**
- Expand targeted nutrition, immunization, and sanitation programs
- Improve healthcare accessibility for refugee populations

**Equity-Based Monitoring**
- Strengthen monitoring systems to track disparities between host and refugee populations
- Utilize disaggregated indicators for resource allocation and intervention targeting

**Multisectoral Coordination**
- Address poverty, food insecurity, inadequate housing, and social vulnerability through integrated programming approaches
